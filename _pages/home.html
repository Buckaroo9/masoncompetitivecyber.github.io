Welcome

Mason Competitive Cyber is a cybersecurity organization at GMU that works towards their members reaching a skilled, qualified state in cybersecurity largely through training and subsequent competition.

We don't expect any prior experience from our members, but we try to accomodate all tiers of technical ability.

Want to Join?

As we don't have any strict membership requirements, the best move is to attend meetings if you can and join our chat directly below whether you can make meetings or not and you can talk to us about what you'd like out of the club and you can still join up for competitions (see #ctf-watch in chat).

So What Actual Competitions?

In addition to competitions listed on CTFtime.org we intend to consider events listed on our meetings and competition page.
We also have not ruled out throwing our own events and have spoken with the IT Support Center and related student organizations on necessary approvals to have an attack/defense CTF. We are mostly just waiting for a related engineering organization to get an infrastructure proposal passed so we can request a portion of it temporarily.

Club Updates

Regular updates on Club News, Events, and Meetings


Mason CC participating in GRIMM HAX

Mason CC is happy to announce we expect to be participating in GRIMM's HAX program starting with their October challenge. This will give us the opportunity to join with other clubs in the program such as RIT's and PSU-Altoona's in challenges that teach a variety of necessary skills, while still challenging the advanced members.



Mason CC wins Booz Allen CTF

Mason CC placed very well in the Booz Allen CTF (see our previous release for details regarding the competition). Pictured to the right is Michael Bailey (President) who won first and in the center is Paul Benoit (Co-President) who won second. In addition, we had a variety of high ranking members, with multiple others in the top 10. More pictures are available on our Facebook page.
