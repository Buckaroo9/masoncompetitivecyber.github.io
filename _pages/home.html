Welcome

Mason Competitive Cyber is a cybersecurity organization at George Mason University with the intention of spreading cybersecurity education and workforce development largely through training and subsequent competition.
We don't expect any prior experience from our members, but we try to accomodate all tiers of technical ability.

So What Actual Competitions?

In addition to competitions listed on CTFtime.org we intend to consider events listed on our meetings and competition page.
We also have not ruled out throwing our own events and have spoken with the IT Support Center and related student organizations on necessary approvals to have an attack/defense CTF. We are mostly just waiting for a related engineering organization to get an infrastructure proposal passed so we can request a portion of it temporarily.

Mason CC wins Booz Allen CTF

Mason CC placed very well in the Booz Allen CTF (see our previous release for details regarding the competition). Pictured to the right is Michael Bailey (President) who won first and in the center is Paul Benoit (Co-President) who won second. In addition, we had a variety of high ranking members, with multiple others in the top 10. More pictures are available on our Facebook page.



Mason CC to host talk by Sam Williams (murmus)

Mason Competitive Cyber will host Sam Williams of Kudu Dynamics in our advanced track, which will take place in JC Room G (what was previously the basic room), for our next meeting on September 27th from 5-6pm. All are welcome, but some of this content may be advanced. We're more than happy to answer any questions on our Slack.



Mason CC kicked off semester September 6th

Mason Competitive Cyber had their first meeting of the semester Wednesday, September 6th, in JC Room D (basic track) and SUB1 3B (advanced track) with a great deal of success. Both rooms experienced higher visitor counts than expected. Both meeting slidedecks and an advanced session recording are available to members per request. The next meeting will be September 13th, though the room remains slightly uncertain.

Any questions about the organization can be directed at Michael Bailey on Mason CC's Slack. Sign up HERE with your GMU address or email us at masoncc@gmu.edu if you're having any issues.
