No Formal Requirements

We do not have a formal list as to who is and isn't a member. GetConnected requests to join our university roster are automatically accepted. We ask that we have some record of your participation, whether that's a presence in our Slack or a competition attendance or something else, before you list you are a CC member on your resume or elsewhere. Otherwise we will not verify this.

Competing

Competitions are open to all. If we don't want you to compete in a particular event and you sign up, we will let you know in direct messages. Registration occurs in Slack, starting in particular the #ctf-watch channel. All competitions generally break off into new channels from ctf-watch to new event channels, for instance #umdctf for UMDCTF, which are announced in ctf-watch along with event ddetails. Carpooling, event details including context we may have on the challenges and social elements are then organized in that channel. If there are mulitple teams from CC competing against each other, they may break off from that to private channels. If not, the competition is generally discussed there. We have an archive of public Slack communications available per request that includes public competition channels.

Social

People regularly communicate at meetings, as well as in Slack above. CC has regular socials, including a WarGames screening, DefCon video potluck, and more. CC also goes out for dinner (or whatever is applicable, if we're in a hotel for a multi-day event we'll obviously do more) generally after a competition for those who competed. Social engagement is something we are always looking to further develop, with plans to do a fundraiser at Panera or Manhattan Pizza.

Learning

In addition to resources passed in Slack, we encourage you to attend our meetings enumerated here. We encourage you to check out whatever topic you find most interesting if there are multiple. There is also a document on getting started that includes educational resources.
